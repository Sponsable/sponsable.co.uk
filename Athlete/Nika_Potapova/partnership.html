Her commitment to you
All of it.
Monthly reporting
A written update every month — what she posted, what performed, what's coming. Includes her five best brand-integrated posts with context. No chasing required.
Responsive and reachable
Replies to emails. Shows up to calls. If something changes that affects a deliverable, she tells you before it becomes a problem — not after.
Deliverables on time
Agreed outputs get delivered on the agreed schedule. If a deadline needs to move, she asks — she doesn't just miss it.
Honest feedback, privately first
If something isn't working — a product, a brief, a direction — she'll say so to you before it surfaces anywhere else. That's not a liability, it's the feedback loop most brands pay to suppress.
Will come to you
Open to visiting brand headquarters, attending product launches, and getting properly acquainted with what a brand makes and why. She wants to understand the product, not just wear it.
What she needs from you
How to make
it work.
01
A clear brief. What the brand stands for, what success looks like, what you actually want from the partnership. She'll do more with direction than without it.
02
Genuine product fit. If the product doesn't belong in her world she won't take the partnership. If it does, she'll be one of the most credible advocates you have.
03
Creative respect. She has an eye for how things should look and she'll want a say in shoots and visual direction. Brands that treat this as a collaboration get more than brands that hand her a brief and a product.
04
Long-term thinking. She is not looking for a one-season arrangement. The value compounds over time — she wants a brand that understands that.
