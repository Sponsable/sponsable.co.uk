What a partnership
looks like.

Nika is looking for paid partnerships with brands whose products genuinely belong in her world. In exchange she'll integrate your product into her content, show up at competitions wearing it, report on what's performing, and treat the relationship as a collaboration rather than a transaction.

Shoot days, film associations, exclusivity, and long-term arrangements are all open to discussion. The starting point is a conversation.

Her commitment
to you.
Monthly reporting
What she posted, what performed, what's coming. Includes her best brand-integrated posts with context.
Responsive and reachable
Replies to emails. Shows up to calls. If something changes, she tells you before it becomes a problem.
Deliverables on time
Agreed outputs on the agreed schedule. If anything changes, she says so in advance.
Honest feedback
If something isn't working — a product, a brief, a direction — she'll say so directly.
Will come to you
Open to visiting brand headquarters and attending product launches. She wants to understand the product, not just wear it.
What she needs
from you.
01
A clear brief. What the brand stands for, what success looks like, what you actually want. She'll do more with direction than without it.
02
Genuine product fit. If it doesn't belong in her world she won't take it. If it does, she'll be one of the most credible advocates you have.
03
Creative respect. She'll want a say in shoots and visual direction. Brands that treat this as a collaboration get more than brands that hand her a brief and a product.
04
Long-term thinking. She is not looking for a one-season arrangement. The value compounds over time.
