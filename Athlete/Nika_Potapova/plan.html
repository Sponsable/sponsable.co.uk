The idea
Not a highlights reel.
Not a gear ad.

Two days with Nika. Some climbing, some conversation. No script, no performance, no stylist. Just a camera and enough time for her to stop thinking about the camera.

There's a version of Nika most people haven't seen. A junior who dominated a sport before she was old enough to drive. A teenager who won a world title and a European championship in the same year she climbed 9a. And then a few years that were quietly difficult — a coach, a war, a relocation — while she kept competing, kept building, and kept her mouth shut.

"I think it's time to say some of it."

This is that film.

Format Short film, 5–10 minutes
Shoot 1–2 days · Paris + crag location
Window Pre-season · Now – April 2026
Status In development · Looking for a partner
What made you want to do this?
I've never really told the full story. People see the results but not what was happening around them. I think there's value in saying it honestly rather than waiting until I'm retired.
What's the film actually about?
Climbing, obviously. But also about what it takes to stay in the sport when things fall apart around you. I kept competing through all of it. That's the part I want people to understand.
Are you worried about being too honest?
No. I know what I want to say and what I don't. The camera doesn't have to know everything.
