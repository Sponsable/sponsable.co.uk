Now you know who she is. Here's what working with her looks like.

This page is for brands considering a partnership. It covers what Nika offers, what she's looking for, and what she commits to. It's designed to answer the questions brands actually ask — not the ones that look good on a pitch deck.

In her own words

"I want to work with a brand that knows what it stands for. Tell me what you need, and I'll be honest about whether I can give it. I'll show up, I'll report back, I'll tell you if something isn't working. I just want it to feel like something real — not me wearing a logo and disappearing."

What she brings
Creative involvement
Nika has an eye for photography and wants a say in how she's represented visually. For the right brand this isn't a complication — it's an asset. She'll contribute to shoot concepts, location choices, and creative direction. The work comes out better for it.
Product testing and feedback
Finishing a degree in coaching and sports management. She tests gear with the context to understand it — and the honesty to tell you what she actually thinks. Private feedback before anything goes public.
Existing media track record
Red Bull Natural Heights (Dec 2025). Red Bull Dual Ascent 2023–24 with Jacob Schubert. She is not a prospect for media work — she has done it at a high level and knows how it works.
A comeback season in progress
Competition season opens May 2026. Spain trip planned for summer — Santa Linya and Siurana. A brand that commits now gets the whole arc, not a retrospective highlight reel.
A story that works beyond climbing
Ukrainian. Refugee card. Competing through displacement, finishing a degree, making a comeback. There is a human narrative here that reaches audiences well outside the climbing community.

Her commitment to you
The things brands lose sleep over. Here's how she handles them.
Monthly reporting
A written update every month — what she posted, what performed, what's coming. Includes her five best brand-integrated posts with context on why she chose them. No chasing required.
Responsive and reachable
Replies to emails. Shows up to calls. If something changes in her schedule that affects a deliverable, she tells you before it becomes a problem — not after.
Deliverables on time
Agreed outputs get delivered on the agreed schedule. If a deadline needs to move, she asks — she doesn't just miss it and go quiet.
Honest feedback, privately first
If something isn't working — a product, a brief, a direction — she'll say so to you before it surfaces anywhere else. That's not a liability. It's the feedback loop most brands pay to suppress.
Will come to you
Open to visiting brand headquarters, attending product launches, and getting properly acquainted with what a brand makes and why. She wants to understand the product, not just wear it.
What she needs from you

The best partnerships are two-way. This is what makes them work from her side.

01
A clear brief. What the brand stands for, what success looks like, what you actually want from the partnership. She'll do more with direction than without it.
02
Genuine product fit. If the product doesn't belong in her world she won't take the partnership. If it does, she'll be one of the most credible advocates you have.
03
Creative respect. She has an eye for how things should look and she'll want a say in shoots and visual direction. The output is better for it. Brands that treat this as a collaboration get more than brands that hand her a brief and a product.
04
Long-term thinking. She is not looking for a one-season arrangement. The value compounds over time — she wants a brand that understands that.
