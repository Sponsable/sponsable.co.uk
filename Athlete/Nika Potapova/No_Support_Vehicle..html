01 — The Athlete

She manages everything herself. The training, the travel, the deals, the logistics. All of it.

I'm Nika Potapova — a professional sport climber from Kyiv, currently based in Paris. I've competed on the World Cup circuit for several years, reached 9a, won a Youth World Championship gold, and been featured in Red Bull's Natural Heights. I've done all of it without a coach, a manager, or any federation funding.

I organise my own travel — often by calling friends to crash on sofas to make it to competitions. I write my own training plans. I make my own calls on nutrition, recovery, and race strategy. I moved to Paris alone this year, still recovering from surgery, with almost nothing in my pocket, because I decided that's where I needed to be. That's not a hardship story. That's just how I operate.

No Support Vehicle is what that actually looks like, documented properly for the first time.

"When I climb I'm just somewhere in another world. I don't hear anything. It's just my peaceful moment."

02 — The Series

Six episodes. One season. No crew. No safety net.

No Support Vehicle is a six-episode long-form documentary series running across the 2025/26 competitive season, culminating at European Championships in August 2026. It follows a single question: what does it actually take to run a World Cup campaign alone, from the inside?

The format is deliberately intimate. Mostly self-shot. Close to the real texture of the thing — the train to a competition she organised herself, the hostel room the night before, the isolation tent before finals, the call home after a bad result. The kind of footage that doesn't usually exist because athletes with teams around them don't have to think about it this way.

This is not a climbing film. It's not a brand film. It's the thing that happens between the competitions — which is almost always the more interesting story.
